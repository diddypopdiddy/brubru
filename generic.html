ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE GUIDELINES

Protect Confidential Data

Do not enter data classified as confidential (PII, student data, medical information, etc.) into publicly-available Generative AI tools, in accordance with the district’s existing policies and FERPA guidelines (BOE Policy 8335). Information shared with Generative AI tools using default settings is not private and could expose proprietary or sensitive information to unauthorized parties.

Adhere to Existing Academic Policy

Review Kingsway’s student handbooks and plagiarism policy (BOE Policy 5701). Faculty should communicate permitted uses, if any, of generative AI for any academic assignment or learning activity. Students are also encouraged to ask their instructors for clarification about the permitted use of AI.

Review Generated Content

AI-generated content can be inaccurate, misleading, biased, entirely fabricated (sometimes called “hallucinations”) or may contain copyrighted material. You are responsible for any content/products you publish that include AI-generated material. Cross reference AI-generated content with trusted and creditable sources.

Questions/Concerns?

Continue following digital literacy best practices, and for any inquiries or uncertainties about the usage or implementation of artificial intelligence, please submit a support ticket to the Technology Department or Instructional Technology Coaches for additional assistance and guidance. Educators are also encouraged to engage in discourse related to the use of AI to enhance student learning outcomes with their PLT and Instructional Supervisor.
